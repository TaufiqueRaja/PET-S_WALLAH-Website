Guard Dogs

Guard dogs are trained to protect property or individuals.

Guard dogs receive specialized training in protection and may also receive obedience training.

Guard dogs may be more aloof or suspicious of strangers.

Guard dogs may have lower exercise and socialization needs, as they are primarily focused on protection.

Owning a guard dog may come with additional legal responsibilities, such as liability for any injuries the dog may cause while protecting the property or individuals.

Pet Dogs

Pet dogs are generally kept for companionship.

Pet dogs typically receive obedience training and may also receive training in specialized activities such as agility or therapy work.

Pet dogs are generally more friendly and approachable.

Pet dogs may have higher exercise and socialization needs, as they are typically kept as companions and may require more attention and interaction.

Owning a guard dog may come with additional legal responsibilities, such as liability for any injuries the dog may cause while protecting the property or individuals.

It's important to consider these differences when deciding whether a pet or guard dog is the right fit for your needs and lifestyle.
